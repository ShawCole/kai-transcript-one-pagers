B2B vs B2C Campaign Playbook

A side-by-side framework for building high-performance campaigns across business and consumer markets.

⚖
Side-by-Side Comparison
Dimension B2C B2B
Decision Maker Individual consumer Committee (Buyer + Champion + End User)
Sales Cycle Hours to days (low ticket), weeks (high ticket) Weeks to months, 6-12mo for enterprise
Touchpoints 7-11 (lower ticket = fewer) 11-14+ (more stakeholders, more research)
Purchase Driver Emotional, impulse, pain-driven Rational + emotional (career risk, trust, ROI)
Channel Strategy Meta (primary), DSP display, email if national Cold email + DSP, Meta, LinkedIn
Creative Approach One avatar, pain-point driven, scrappy (UGC-style) Multiple avatars per ICP, role-specific, polished
Copy Focus Benefits, urgency, social proof ROI, risk reduction, competitive comparison
Avatars 1-2 per product 3+ per ICP (Buyer, Champion, End User)
Landing Pages Fewer, conversion-optimized Multiple per avatar, education-heavy
ABM Approach Not applicable Essential — target multiple roles per company
⚙
Campaign Architecture

B2C Architecture

  • ICP ≈ Avatar — the person IS the target
  • Fewer variations needed, more frequency acceptable
  • Emotion-first creative with clear CTA
  • Lower ticket = fewer touchpoints to close
  • Scrappy / authentic creative often outperforms polished

B2B Architecture

  • ICP = The business (firmographic: revenue, headcount, industry)
  • Avatar = The person (role: CXO, VP, manager, end user)
  • Each avatar gets own ad set, copy, and landing page
  • Awareness stages per avatar: Problem → Solution → Product Aware
  • Keywords differ by role for the same underlying problem

"Have Two Different Brains"

B2B and B2C require fundamentally different strategies, creative assets, channels, and timelines. Never apply a B2C playbook to B2B or vice versa.
