#19
"M1 is money supply, M2 has to do with velocity of money"
Reality: M1 and M2 are both money supply measures. M1 = cash + checking deposits. M2 = M1 + savings + money market + small CDs. Velocity (V) is a separate metric: GDP ÷ Money Supply.

Velocity of Money Analogy — Better Alternative

Shaw's analogy (money circulates → touchpoints compound) works at surface level but breaks down: ad spend is consumed, not re-circulated.

Better analogy: Marketing Compound Interest. Each touchpoint compounds on previous ones. First impression = minimal impact. Fifth = recognition. Tenth = trust. Twelfth = action. Multi-channel = diversified portfolio reducing risk while maintaining compounding growth.
