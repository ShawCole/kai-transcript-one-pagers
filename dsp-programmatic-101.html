The Basics

What is a DSP?

A Demand Side Platform enables buying ad inventory across the open web through real-time bidding. This is everything OUTSIDE of Meta and Google's "walled gardens."

Walled Gardens
  • Meta — Facebook, Instagram, Threads, Messenger
  • Google — Search, YouTube, Gmail, Display Network
  • Closed ecosystems with proprietary data
  • Buy only through their platforms
  • Limited transparency & portability
VS
The Open Web (DSP)
  • News sites — NYT, CNN, Forbes, etc.
  • Apps & Games — mobile in-app inventory
  • Streaming & CTV — Roku, Hulu, Pluto
  • Blogs, forums, niche sites
  • Accessed via ad exchanges through DSP
Inventory Sources

These are the marketplaces where publishers sell ad inventory and DSPs bid to place ads.

Largest and most powerful exchange. Premium demand, highest fill rates, top CPMs for publishers.
Magnite

Index Exchange
Transparent, high-performance exchange. Display, video, CTV, and mobile with real-time analytics.
PubMatic
Advanced analytics platform. Strong mobile presence with identity solutions for audience targeting.
Xandr

Amazon Publisher Services
Amazon's full ad tech stack. Unique access to Amazon shopper data and purchase intent signals.
Sharethrough
Specializes in native and display formats that blend with content for higher engagement rates.
Smaato
Mobile-focused exchange. Strong in-app inventory with global reach across mobile publishers.

Production

Creative Requirements Per Campaign

3-5 variations per size per avatar. Structured in tiers to maximize coverage and performance.

Tier 1 — Core

Primary Display

3-5 variations across 3 primary sizes (Leaderboard, Medium Rectangle, Half Page). These carry the campaign.

Tier 2 — Extended

Secondary Display

3 variations across 3 secondary sizes (Wide Skyscraper, Large Rectangle, Mobile Banner). Broader reach.

Tier 3 — Video

Brand Awareness

Video creative for brand awareness and product-aware prospects. Pre-roll and outstream formats.

Rotation cadence: Swap creatives every 2-4 weeks to combat creative fatigue. Monitor CTR decay as a signal to refresh.
Mechanics

How Bidding Works

Ad impressions are bought and sold through automated auctions that happen in milliseconds.

⚡

Real-Time Bidding (RTB)

Open auctions where every impression is bid on in milliseconds. You set bid price, targeting criteria, and budget. The highest bidder wins the impression.

🔒

Private Marketplace (PMP)

Invite-only auctions with select publishers. Better inventory quality and pricing predictability with preferred access to premium placements.

📄

Programmatic Guaranteed

Fixed-price, reserved inventory deals. Guaranteed impressions with specific publishers at negotiated rates. Most predictable, highest commitment.

Operations

Critical Operational Concepts

The non-negotiable operational elements that separate effective campaigns from wasted budget.

Frequency Capping

Limit impressions per user per day. Without this, you waste budget showing the same ad 100x to one person. Typical cap: 3-5 impressions per user per day.

Brand Safety

Use allowlists and blocklists to prevent ads appearing on low-quality or harmful sites. Protect brand reputation by controlling placement context.

Fraud & Bot Detection

Layer verification tools (IAS, DoubleVerify, MOAT) to filter bot traffic. Intent data alone does not solve this — dedicated fraud detection is required.

Viewability

Track whether ads were actually seen — above the fold, in-view for 1+ second. Industry benchmark: 70%+ viewability rate. Below that, investigate placements.

Cookie Deprecation

Third-party cookies are being phased out across browsers. This affects targeting precision and measurement attribution. Evaluate contextual targeting, first-party data strategies, and unified ID solutions as alternatives. This is a critical strategic consideration for any DSP investment.
